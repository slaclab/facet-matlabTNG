The LUCRETIA Project

Welcome to Lucretia


Lucretia is a Matlab-based physics toolbox for the simulation of high-performance single-pass electron beam transport systems. It was initially designed to support studies of Linear Collider Low Emittance Transport (bunch compressor, linear accelerator, final focus), and also the transport and acceleration system of linac-driven free electron lasers.

Lucretia Author List:  The original Lucretia code was written by P. Tenenbaum, SLAC. The current maintenance crew for Lucretia are:

       Glen White, SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory, CA, USA
       Mark Woodley, SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory, CA, USA

The management gratefully acknowledges the assistance of Paul Emma, Tor Raubenheimer, Andrei Terebilo, and Andy Wolski.  The authors originally got the idea for a beam dynamics code which was a library of functions rather than a self-contained program from Nick Walker, whom we mocked for it back in 1994; but in the end, Nick got the last laugh.
